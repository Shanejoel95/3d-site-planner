# Research Checklist

How to build a complete picture of a client. Combine what the user gave you with
what you fetch. For each source: how to find it, what to pull, and what it tells
you about the 3D-site opportunity.

## 1. Existing website
**Find:** user-provided URL, or `web_search` for `"<business> <location>" official site`.
**Fetch:** home + 3–5 key pages (about, services/products, contact, any portfolio).
Use `web_fetch` — don't audit from snippets.
**Pull:**
- What they offer, primary call-to-action, target customer.
- Visual maturity: design quality, photography, brand consistency.
- Tech signals: platform/CMS, page weight, load feel, mobile handling, any existing
  motion/3D.
- Content depth: is copy thin or rich? Testimonials present?
- Conversion path: how does a visitor actually become a lead/customer?
**Tells you:** baseline to beat, what to keep, what's actively hurting them, and how
ambitious the jump to 3D can be.

**If a site exists, you are in Mode A (preserve) — capture, don't just assess.**
Inventory the real content so Phase 4 can reuse it verbatim instead of inventing:
- **Copy:** exact headlines, section text, product/service names, prices, CTAs,
  contact/legal info — captured word-for-word.
- **Structure:** the actual list of pages and the sections within each, in order.
- **Assets:** logo, exact brand colors (hex), fonts, and the URLs of photography,
  product images, and video to be reused.
This inventory is the fixed input for the build — the 3D layer animates it; it does
not rewrite it. (See the content-fidelity contract in SKILL.md.)

## 2. No website found
Not a dead end — usually the biggest opportunity. Record explicitly:
- They're starting from scratch → brand, content, and photography all in scope.
- Lean harder on reviews, social, and competitors to infer positioning.
- The plan should budget for foundational work (messaging, asset creation) before
  3D polish.

## 3. Google reviews & ratings
**Find:** `web_search` for `<business> <location> reviews` / `google reviews`.
**Pull:** star rating, review count, recurring praise themes, recurring complaints,
standout customer phrases (paraphrase — never paste long quotes).
**Tells you:**
- High rating + volume → make social proof a hero element (a reviews/trust scene).
- Recurring praise → headline messaging angles grounded in real perception.
- Recurring complaints → things the site must counter or reassure about.
- Few/no reviews → trust-building is a site goal, not an asset to amplify.

## 4. News, articles & press
**Find:** `web_search` for `<business> news`, `<business> award`, `<founder> interview`.
**Pull:** awards, milestones, founder story, credibility markers, controversies.
**Tells you:** authority hooks for copy, "as featured in" elements, story angles for
an immersive narrative section.

## 5. Video (YouTube / social)
**Find:** `web_search`/YouTube for `<business>` channel or videos.
**Pull:** do they have usable footage? Production quality? Brand personality on camera?
**Tells you:** whether video can anchor scenes, and the brand's tone/energy.

## 6. Photography & visual assets
**Pull from site/social:** product shots, location/team photos, logo, brand colors,
typography.
**Assess:** resolution, consistency, whether assets are 3D-ready (clean product shots
model well; cluttered snapshots don't).
**Tells you:** the single biggest driver of 3D feasibility. Strong assets → showcase
in 3D. Weak/none → budget a shoot, source stock, or generate (AI/3D-modeled) assets.

## 7. Social profiles
**Find:** Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, TikTok by name.
**Pull:** follower scale, posting cadence, engagement, visual style, audience tone.
**Tells you:** audience demographics, brand voice, and how much living content exists
to feed the site.

## 8. Competitors
**Find:** `web_search` for `<industry> <location> competitors` or top players in niche.
**Fetch:** 2–3 competitor sites.
**Pull:** their visual bar, whether anyone uses 3D/motion, positioning gaps.
**Tells you:** how to differentiate and where a 3D site creates a real edge.

## 9. Signals → opportunities cheat sheet
| Signal found | Plan implication |
|---|---|
| 4.5★+, many reviews | Social-proof hero / trust scene |
| Strong product photography | 3D product showcase / configurator |
| No website | From-scratch brand + content + photography in scope |
| Thin or generic copy | Messaging workshop; copy is a deliverable |
| Slow / heavy legacy site | Performance is a primary, stated goal |
| Distinctive brand identity | Signature custom hero interaction (lean Three.js) |
| Tiny budget / DIY maintenance | 3D-lite or Spline, not custom WebGL |
| Competitors all flat/templated | 3D becomes the core differentiator |
| Regulated / accessibility needs | Strong non-3D fallback is mandatory |
